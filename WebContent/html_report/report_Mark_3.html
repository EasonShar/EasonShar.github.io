Hilda also played a significant role in fundraising for excavations - she was one of two Honorary Secretaries for the British School of Archaeology in Egypt, gathering subscriptions to send Petrie's students to Egypt to obtain excavation experience. Her contributions to publicising and fundraising were marked. She lectured extensively at women's clubs and literary societies, and by the 1930s she was giving radio broadcasts on the excavations in Palestine.
